# Why a portfolio?
* I've been looking for employment and this is one area in which I'm lacking. I got lost a bit in the search and have neglected doing. I'd like to change that, and have something to show off as proof of what I can do, and to act as a point of discussion. Not to mention it's fun to make stuff!

# Why is this starting from scratch now?
A lot of my learning and practice has been doing work on top of the output of a tutorial or something [such as this one](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TtPXvEcE11E). As such, a lot of a assets and code isn't _really_ my own so I haven't felt like I've wanted to publish it.
All of the code that I've worked on professionally has been under NDA or for enterprise and isn't something I can share either.

# What should a portfolio contain?
* General information about me as a person. Think digital CV.
 * Do I want to include any identifyable information such as a name? First name is okay and can be inferred from my github username!
 * Do I keep it purely professional or slip in some personality too? I think the latter.
* Projects that achieve at least 1 (preferably all!) of the following:
 * Show off or practice / reinforce a skill I have,
 * Learn and build a new skill (think language, technology, process, pattern, etc),
 * Be something cool and useful to myself or someone else, or
 * Just be really fun to work on!
